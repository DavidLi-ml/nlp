In addition to feeding on seeds and flowers, some parrot species prey on animals, especially invertebrate larvae. Golden-winged parakeets prey on water snails, and the kea of New Zealand kills juvenile petrels and adult sheep. Another New Zealand parrot, the Antipodes parakeet, enters the burrows of nesting grey-backed storm petrels and kills the incubating adults. Some cockatoos and the kākā excavate branches and wood to obtain grubs; the bulk of the yellow-tailed black cockatoo's diet is made up of insects
