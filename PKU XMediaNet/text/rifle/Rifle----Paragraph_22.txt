The Russo-Japanese War of 1904-1905 was the first modern war of the 20th century. Military observers from Great Britain, Germany, France, and the United States witnessed first hand the first major conflict fought with high velocity bolt-action rifles firing smokeless powder on a massive scale. The Battle of Mukden fought in 1905 consisted of nearly 343,000 Russian troops against over 281,000 Japanese troops.
