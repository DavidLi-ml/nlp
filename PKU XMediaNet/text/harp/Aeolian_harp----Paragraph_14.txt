In Negrar, in province of Verona (Italy), you can see a modern monument Aeolian harp more than six meters high. It is a sound sculpture designed by the Italian artist Giuseppe Ferlenga which was inaugurated in November 2015 from the Sports and Cultural Group of Mazzano. The acoustic part of this tool is composed of a frame that contains a copper harmonic case. The Aeolian Harp of Negrar has six strings of different lengths and materials. If there is wind, this monumental harp produces audible sounds up to a distance of about four meters.
