In the 1670s, the French devised the copper pontoon; after this point, rivers and canals ceased to present significant obstacles. The early modern period in pontoon use was dominated by the wars of the 18th and 19th centuries during which the art and science of pontoon bridging barely changed. This however did not stop all innovation, in 1708 a Swedish army used a leather pontoon bridge to cross a river before the Battle of Holowczyn
